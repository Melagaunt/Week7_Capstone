Disc Golf

Definitions

A game, much like Golf, however we use discs and baskets to score. The execution and goal is much the same as well, were you start at a tee-off area, and throw your disc down the course to the next basket.

A disc, is similar in shape and size to a frisbee, but can vary greatly in weight and design. A basket, is typically a pole with an elevated basin & chains.

Rules of the Game

Much like golf, our goal is to complete the course in as few strokes as possible. A typically game is 9, or 18 holes.

Tee off order is settled by lowest score on the prior hole. On hole 1, this can be a rock-paper-scisors, or a coin flip.

Players take turns throwing their discs in the general direction of the basket. Begining with the disc farthest from the basket.

Discs are launched from the lie of the previous throw. Follow through steps are allowed on all throws, outside of a 10ft putting range.

Lowest number of throws wins the hole, and earns the right to tee-off first

Pack it in, pack it out.
Try to leave your course better than you found it.

Hazards are to be avoided, whenever possible. These can lead to extra stokes on your score, or even lost or damaged discs.

Trees: If a disc is in a tree out of reach, place a marker below it while you attempt to retrieve. This will be your subsequent launch area

Water: Rivers and lakes add to a beautiful experience. A disc lost in water however, will sink, and potentially float away. Best to avoid.

Mandatory:This is a required flight path. These are generally indicated at the tee, and serve the purpose to add difficulty or protect the natural landscape.
